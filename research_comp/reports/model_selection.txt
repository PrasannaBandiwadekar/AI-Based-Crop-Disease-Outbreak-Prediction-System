🔬 V5 Final Training & Evaluation Strategy
With the calendar grid and missing-data semantics strictly resolved, the dataset now perfectly mirrors the physical reality of the sugarcane microclimate. Here is the final strategy for modeling and evaluating the system.

1. Data Assumption Validation Layer
Rainfall (Event-Based): By switching to fillna(0), rainfall remains a sparse, discrete physical event. Accumulations (PREC_sum14) are no longer artificially inflated by forward-fill, meaning 100mm of accumulated rain strictly equals 100mm of observed precipitation.
Temperature & Humidity (Continuous Fields): Using interpolate(method='linear', limit=3) respects atmospheric thermodynamics. A 3-day gap between 29°C and 32°C will smoothly transition (30°C, 31°C), which perfectly mirrors the actual gradual warming/cooling of air masses.
Physical Consistency: Confirmed. The feature space is now mathematically incapable of hallucinating false meteorological states.
2. Model Selection Strategy (CRITICAL)
Model Type	Suitability for Mixed/Sparse Physics	Interpretability for Agronomists	Verdict
Random Forest	Good. Handles non-linear biological thresholds well, but struggles slightly with highly sparse features (like zero-filled rain).	High. Feature importance is native and easy to extract.	Strong Baseline.
XGBoost / LightGBM	Excellent. Natively optimizes for sparsity (zero-filled rainfall is handled optimally). Capable of learning sharp threshold bounds (e.g., exactly 29-33°C).	High (with SHAP). SHAP values provide exact contribution metrics for each daily decision.	🏆 Primary Choice.
LSTM / GRU	Poor. While great at sequence modeling, deep learning requires vast data to prevent overfitting. We have ~7,000 rows. It will overfit to noise.	Low. "Black box" nature will severely hurt trust with farmers and agronomists.	Reject for V5.
3. Feature Interaction Stability Test
env_interaction_score Validation: With rainfall inflation removed, achieving a score of 3 now requires a true intersection of high heat, massive real rainfall, and sustained humidity. It has shifted from a highly sensitive (noisy) metric to a highly specific, high-confidence signal.
Rolling Humidity + Rainfall Coupling: Realistic. The model will correctly learn that heavy rain followed by a dry breeze (low RH) is safe, but rain followed by stagnant, interpolating high humidity represents severe canopy incubation.
4. Outbreak Sensitivity Calibration (IMPORTANT)
The model must be tuned (via hyperparameter weighting and objective functions) to react physically:

Short Rain Bursts: Should cause a momentary blip, but must NOT trigger an outbreak alert unless RH2M_roll7 remains strictly elevated.
Sustained Humidity Periods: Should act as a "multiplier" on baseline risk. The longer the streak, the exponentially higher the risk becomes when rain eventually hits.
Temperature Stability Windows: Should act as a strict hard-gate. The model must learn that regardless of rain and humidity, if T2M_roll7 drops below 25°C or spikes above 35°C, Red Rot fungal activity ceases.
5. Evaluation Design (TIME-SERIES CORRECT)
Validation Methodology: Forward Chaining (Walk-Forward Validation) No random shuffling. The temporal arrow must be preserved.

Fold 1: Train (2005-2018) → Val (2019-2021)
Fold 2: Train (2005-2021) → Test (2022-2024)
(Optional) Region Transfer Test: If data is available, train on Bihar and validate pure transferability to Uttar Pradesh or Maharashtra to prove the model learned physics, not local geography.
Metrics Hierarchy:

Recall (Outbreak Detection): Primary. The cost of missing a Red Rot outbreak is total crop loss. We must catch 95%+ of biological outbreak windows.
Lead-Time Accuracy: Must reliably trigger HIGH risk strictly between 3 and 7 days before the validated peak.
False Positive Rate (FPR): Secondary, but critical for farmer trust. The monsoon_flag suppression will keep this heavily controlled.
6. Final System Architecture Recommendation
Best Model Choice: XGBoost Classifier heavily weighted to minority positive classes (scale_pos_weight), paired with a SHAP Explainer layer to generate English-language advisories (e.g., "High risk detected due to sustained 88% humidity over the last 7 days").
Deep Learning Needed: NO. Tabular, threshold-driven tree architectures (XGBoost) are mathematically superior for bounded physical environments with ~10k rows.
Production Readiness Verdict: YES. With data leakage, temporal misalignment, and missing-value hallucinations fully eradicated, the pipeline is architecturally sound and ready for model fitting.
